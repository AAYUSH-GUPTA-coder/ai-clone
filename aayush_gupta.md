The thesis was that crypto's settlement layer is broken — solvers and bridges move gross capital rather than netting flows, which wastes capital and creates fragility. Over 13 months we signed LOIs with two solver networks bridging $10B+ annually, won Axelar Infinity Quest, finished runner-up at the QuickNode Accelerator, were finalists at Base Batches, received grants from Axelar and Base, and earned an incubation slot in W3X Batch 07.
 
Alongside building, I've written continuously — 35+ tutorials and 150k+ developer reads, including the foundational EVM internals reference series for **Cyfrin Audits** and earlier work for **Integral, QuickNode, DeveloperDAO, and LearnWeb3DAO**.
 
I've now made the call to wind down SettleX as my primary venture. Fundraising conditions for institutional infra are tough, and I want to ship from a position of clarity rather than grind on a runway that won't close. I'm actively exploring two parallel tracks: (1) start my new startup, and (2) a job search for senior smart contract, DevRel, or technical writing roles at strong remote-friendly teams.
 
The throughline of my career is that I am most useful where **deep smart contract engineering meets developer education**. I'm now leveling up that combination with AI engineering — Claude Code skills, MCP servers, and agentic systems in Python.
